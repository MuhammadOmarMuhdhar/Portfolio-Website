About Me

Consultant, Computational Scentist, Photographer

I earned my undergraduate degree in Politics, Economics, and Philosophy from the University of Texas at Austin, laying a foundation to comprehend the complexities of our world. Now pursuing a Master's in Computational Social Sciences at UC Berkeley, I aim to infuse a humanistic perspective into the rapidly evolving field of artificial intelligence.

While AI has recently made significant strides, the humanities offer a crucial viewpoint currently absent in the field. Understanding technology's social and philosophical implications is pivotal for creating innovative solutions that address real-world challenges and push the boundaries of what is achievable with Artificial Intelligence.

Before joining Berkeley, I worked as a Data Operations Consultant in Wealth Asset Management, where I streamlined firms' data processes. Beyond my professional pursuits, I engage in photography as a hobby.
